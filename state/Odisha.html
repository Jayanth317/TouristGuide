Bhubaneswar

A perfect amalgamation of old and new cultures, Bhubaneswar is famously deemed as the ‘Temple City of India’. From verdant parks and lakes to the ancient temples and caves, there are plenty of places to visit in Bhubaneswar. The architectural style evident in temples speaks volumes about the engineering prowess of our ancestors. The city is also known for its thriving trade and business.

Puri

Packed with tourist attractions, Puri is a must-visit destination for devotees and leisure travelers. Puri is home to one of the Char Dhams. Indulging in various water activities, bird watching, relaxing by the Bay of Bengal, and paying respects to different deities at the many temples are some of the things that you can do here.

Cuttack

The cultural capital of Odisha, Cuttack is also known as 'Silver City' and 'Millennium City'. It is also popular for ivory filigree, eye-catching silver, and brass works; and for 1000 years of history. From old forts to wildlife sanctuaries, this city has everything to keep history buffs and nature lovers entertained while they are exploring Cuttack.

Konark

The famous Konark Sun Temple is the major reason to visit Konark in Odisha. Admired for its intricately carved architecture around the globe, this temple is dedicated to the Sun God and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Apart from temples, one can also enjoy the serene beaches of Konark.

Chandipur

You must have heard of the disappearing beach at least once, right? Well, this unique phenomenon occurs at the beach of Chandipur. The seawater at this beach keeps receding and coming back during the low tide. You can stroll down the beach into the seabed when there is no water, enjoy scenic sunrise and sunset, and try delectable seafood prepared at the beach.

Dhauli

Popular among the followers of Lord Gautam Buddha, Dhauli is a small town near Bhubaneswar. A deadly battle between the state of Kalinga and King Ashoka’s Maurya Empire took place in Dhauli. The serenity of Daya River adds to the character of this small town, providing some breathtakingly beautiful scenic vistas.

Rayagada

An interesting place to visit Odisha for history buffs, Rayagada is peppered with historical monuments and temples. Native tribal population, handmade products, handicrafts, and delectable cuisine are some of the things that make this place famous amongst travelers. Laxminarayan Temple in Rayagada is known for its spiritual and religious magnificence.

Bhitarkarnika National Park

The second-largest mangrove ecosystem in the country, Bhitarkanika National Park is spread across 672 square kilometers of land. You can take a boat ride on the network of creeks and canals and watch the inhabitants like pythons, deer, hyenas, saltwater crocodiles, wild boars, and migratory birds in their natural habitat.

Kalahandi

A wonderful place to visit in the state, Kalahandi is known for its intriguing history and natural beauty. Popular for housing the traces of human settlement from 500 BC to 500 AD and the remains of an old fort, Asuragarh is a popular tourist destination in this district.

Jajpur

Popular for boasting many pilgrimage sites, old monuments, and archaeological ruins, Jajpur is a district with historic and religious significance. Situated on the banks of the Baitarani River, Jagannath Temple is an archaeological wonder and a major tourist attraction.
