West Godavari

A temple, built by a saint who underwent penance in an anthill, Dwaraka Tirumala is a popular religious place in West Godavari. This temple is also regarded as mini-Tirupati by locals. This district in Andhra Pradesh is also home to an important beach, Perupalem. The river Godavari flows through the district, which remains at its prime beauty throughout the year. There are several water activities in this region available to keep the adventurers satisfied. Some of the top tourist attractions in West Godavari are Pattisam, Eluru, Polavaram, and Kolleru Lake.

Nalgonda

Nalgona district in Andhra Pradesh is known for its popular tourist attractions. A religious place, Yadadri in Nalgonda attracts a huge number of visitors during festivals. The famous Nagarjuna Sagar Dam is located in Nalgonda with many scenic waterfalls and a Buddha museum. Popular for Buddhist museums and structures, Nandkonda is also situated in this district. Nalgonda has a lot of unique and historically important sculptures spread around the region.

Chittoor

A winter destination in Andhra Pradesh, Chittoor receives a large amount of precipitation and the temperature can go down to 16 degrees Celsius in the winter season. This district in the state is surrounded by dense forest. There are not many tourist spots in Chittoor but those who love to spend some time amongst untouched nature will enjoy every second of their holiday in this region. The richest temple in the world, Tirupati Temple is situated in the Chittoor district.

Visakhapatnam

Famous for tourist spots like Dolphin nose hill, sandy beaches, ship ports, and refineries, Visakhapatnam is an important district in Andhra Pradesh. Bhumunipatnam is famous for its Buddhist monasteries ruins, which are located just a short drive away from the index city of Visakhapatnam. Many Dutch-era structures are spread around the region. Andhra’s best-kept secret, Araku Valley is also situated in this district. The valley is a honeymoon destination with lots of waterfalls and hills surrounding the region.

Adilabad

With 50% of the area covered by forest, Adilabad is the second largest district in Andhra Pradesh. With such a vast amount of jungles in the region, it is quite famous for wildlife activities amongst nature lovers. The Godavari River flows through Adilabad, multiplying its beauty even more. Some of the major attractions in this district include Nirmal Fort, Kuntala Waterfall, Pochera Waterfall, and Kawal Wildlife Sanctuary. The sanctuary is home to several wild animals such as long-tailed monkeys, deer, boars, bison, and tiger.

Khammam

If you are looking for some solitary time, then the Khammam district in Andhra Pradesh is perfect for you as it is still unknown to many people. Some of the major attractions in this region are the Godavari River, Khammam Fort, and Kinnerasani Sanctuary. You can find a lot of green fields and horticulture lands here because agriculture is the top economic element of this district. The scenic ghee well, road bridge over Godavari River, and Bogatha Waterfall are must-see attractions in the area.

Medak

With a forest covering around 30 kilometers of area, Medak is famous for its fauna and flora resources. For people who want to enjoy outdoor entertainment time, this district has various scenic places and exotic wildlife spots. To spend a blissful day watching the birds in their natural habitat, don’t forget to visit the 9 islands located in Manjira Wildlife Sanctuary. A temple in a small fort located in Medak district is famous amongst religious people as it was made by carving out a single stone. Thousands of devotees pay homage to this temple during festivals.

Nizamabad

Pocharam Wildlife Sanctuary is an important tourism attraction in the Nizamabad district. A little further away from Nizamabad’s district headquarters, Alisagar gets filled with decorative gardens and deer parks during summer. For trekkers and water sport enthusiasts, Nizamabad is a perfect place. Apart from nature and adventure, Nizamabad has religious importance too as it is home to the oldest Muslim pilgrimage in Badapahad and an old Hanuman temple in the region.

Warangal

A temple made of around a thousand pillars is a must-visit attraction in Warangal. This temple attracts visitors from all over the world. A fort which is now in ruins is also a great tourist spot in this district. The other famous tourist attractions in Warangal are Ramappa Temple, Pakhal Lake, and Medaram. What’s more interesting about this district is that it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visit Bhadrakali Lake during sunset if you want to spend a romantic evening with your loved one.

Kadapa

Home to many tourist attractions. Kadapa district has a Hindu temple in Vontimitta, which was built in the 16th century. This beautiful temple is dedicated to Lord Ram. Apart from this temple, there are other religious places in this district such as Nandalur, Rayachoty, and others. This place is so beautiful that many Bollywood movies were shot here. A 300-year-old shrine, Ameen Peer Dargah is also situated in this region. With many endangered species like Jerdon’s courser, Sri Lankamalleswara wildlife sanctuary is an important wildlife reserve in Kadapa.
