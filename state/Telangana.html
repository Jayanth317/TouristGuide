Hyderabad

The capital city of Telangana and Biryani Capital of India, Hyderabad is also referred to as the City of Pearls. The city is sprinkled with royal forts, historic structures, scenic water bodies, and landscaped parks. Hyderabad, the original home of Koh-i-noor, the most precious diamond which is now sitting in the museum of London was mined from Golconda mines. The highlight of the city and one of the best places in Hyderabad is the huge monolithic Buddha statue standing erect in the center of the Hussain Sagar Lake.

Alampur

Located on the banks of Tungabhadra River, Alampur is a religious destination with a lot of temples dedicated to different Gods and Goddesses. Some of the major temples located here are Sangameshwara Temple, the Nava Brahma Temples, the Jogulamba Temple and more. Alampur is also the gateway to Srisailam, the pilgrimage town in Telangana. These ancient temples date back to the 7th to 16th Centuries and inscriptions on them tell us a lot about their history. Architectural enthusiasts and history buffs would love to explore this place.

Warangal

One of the most treasured destinations of Telangana Tourism, Warangal is famous for its historical splendor and serenity. Despite being situated within the city, Warangal seems to be a lush verdant patch that gives a sense of peace. The camping and soothing attractions of the place will give you a much-desired sense of peace and tranquility. The Pakhal Lake is the perfect place to spend a day away from the hustle and bustle of the city. This calm lake against a beautiful backdrop of hills, Pakhal Wildlife Sanctuary, magnificent forts and temples are worth exploring in Warangal.

Khammam

One of the most scenic spots in Telangana, Khammam is a beautiful place known for rugged forts and sanctuaries. The place was under the rule of several dynasties, including the Nizams of Hyderabad. The glimpses of their rule can be seen in architectural splendors spread all over the place. With River Godavari crisscrossing through the town, the place is worth exploring and enjoying its lovely attractions.

Medak

Known for its awe-inspiring Medak Fort, it is a scenic place that is sure to help you unwind and feel relaxed. Surrounded by verdant picturesque splendors all around, the fort is beautified with Indo-Islamic architectural style and amazing sculptures. The impressive Cathedral Church is also one of the major landmarks worth exploring and checking out. The whole area is enveloped with an eerie and Gothic feel. Combined with architectural brilliance, the fort seems to tell stories of its past to the visitors. Wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers would love exploring the famous Pocharam Wildlife Sanctuary and enjoy witnessing rich biodiversity.

Ananthagiri

A trekker’s paradise, Ananthagiri is a beautiful place, a perfect destination to escape mundane life. The serene and placid lake, surrounding the index temple of Lord Shiva and ruined structures creates a perfect ambiance to unwind, relax, and soak in the marvels of nature spread all around. The Anantha Padmanabha Swamy Temple attracts devotees from far and wide areas to seek blessings of the deity, the Protector of the Universe. Built approximately 400 years ago by Nizams of Hyderabad, this temple envelopes you with a sense of calm and peace. Trekkers can trek and explore the caves and other ruins in the area. Visitors also love Kayaking in the reservoir area.

Mahbubnagar

One of the prominent historical and religious sites in Telangana, Mahbubnagar is also a land where great battles were fought and won. Liberally sprinkled with a lot of forts and ruins, it tells stories about its war-ridden past. It was ruled by several kingdoms like Vijayanagar, Brahmanis, Chalukyas, and Golconda Nawabs. With River Tungabhadra and Krishna flowing through the area, Mahbubnagar looks scenic and endowed with nature’s best attractions. The serene beauty of this place has made Mahbubnagar one of the most important tourist towns in Telangana.

Sangareddy

Located close to Hyderabad, Sangareddy is popular for its Jail Museum. Sprawled in a vast expanse of land, it is divided into three sections. The oldest part of the jail has two sections where male and female wings are located. The male wing has a historical center with nine impressive structures while the female wing has only one structure. This portion has washing space, break room, kitchen, storage spaces, gas room, store apportion room, etc. The third portion has a huge historical center where visitors can know more about tough jail life.

Nizamabad

The center of culture and art, Nizamabad is another one of the several must-visit places in Telangana. Recorded landmarks and stone-engraved sanctuaries attract a lot of tourists to know more about this place and its contribution to art and culture of this state. Kanteshwar, an old sanctuary, is an excellent example of North Indian engineering style. Delicately perched atop a slope, Nizamabad Fort commands mesmerizing and scenic views all around. Plan a picnic with friends or family at Alisagar Reservoir and enjoy magnificent views.

Adilabad

Adilabad is a place known for its scenic beauty and magnificent waterfalls that bring the place alive with lush greenery and melodious sounds of water cascading from a height. Located at the border of Telangana and Maharashtra, this picturesque is a feast for tired souls. The place got its name from its erstwhile ruler, Adil Shah. Today, it is a place known for its repertoire of natural attractions like waterfalls, dense forests, serene rivers, and picturesque valleys.
