Nelong Valley

A rocky desert in the Gangotri National Park, Nelong Valley is perched at a height of 11,000 feet above sea level. The valley was opened for tourists in 2015, and since then it has become a famous destination in Uttarakhand for adventure junkies. Nelong Valley was an essential trade route between India and Tibet before the Chinese occupied Tibet. This valley has similar weather, landscape, and looks exactly like Tibet, Spiti, and Ladakh regions.

Mukteshwar

Mukteshwar is one of the best places to visit in Uttarakhand. Adventure enthusiasts love engaging in activities like rappelling, camping, trekking, rock climbing, and many other adrenaline-pumping activities. The place is blessed with nature's choicest blessings. The magnificent views of the Himalayan Range are a sight to behold. Check out verdant rolling meadows, dense coniferous forests, fruit orchards, plummeting waterfalls, and serene temples. Mukesteshwar is worth a visit when planning a vacay to Uttarakhand.

Sahastradhara

Sahastradhara is located at a distance of 18 km from Dehradun. The place has a lot of waterfalls and caves worth exploring. Water dropping from limestone stalactites is a lovely sight. There is a collection of pools that turns into Sulphur springs. People come here from far and wide areas to cure ailments like the restricted flow of blood, muscle spasms, arthritis, acne, muscle ache, etc. The lukewarm water of these sulphur springs helps to de-stress.

Lachhiwala

One of the best Uttarakhand tourist places, Lachhiwala is a lovely picnic spot away from the hustle and bustle of Dehradun City. It is lovely to see small ponds surrounded by dense Sal trees. People visit this place to admire natural and scenic beauty while enjoying a picnic with family and friends. People also love trekking in this area. There are man-made pools where bathing can be enjoyed. It is also a perfect place to spot some lovely birds.

Tiffin Top

Tiffin Top in Nainital is another attraction worth checking out when visiting Uttarakhand for a brief holiday. It is also popular as Dorothy's Seat as it has a stone bench built by Col. J.P Kellet in memory of Dorothy Kellet, his wife. It is located 2290 m above sea level. Perched atop Ayarpatta Hill, it provides magnificent views of nature's beauty scattered all around. It is a terraced hilltop providing 360-degree views of the Himalayan range and Nanital City. Adventure lovers can engage in activities like trekking, zip swings, etc.

Dharchula

When planning a visit to Uttarakhand, include Dharchula in your list of places to visit in Uttarakhand. It is famous for Askot, located in the Pithoragarh district. Askot, the Himalayan town, is worth visiting and exploring to enjoy its serene beauty. Another attraction worth exploring is Askot Musk Deer Sanctuary, located close to Askot. Other attractions include Chaukori, Om Parvat, Adi Kailash, etc.

Rishikesh

Rishikesh is a spiritual paradise and a hippy’s safe haven set at the foothills of the lofty Garhwal Himalayas. The Ganges cutting right through the city multiplies the charm. The hilly town is one of the most famous places of Uttarakhand as it boasts acres of forests, lush mountains, stunning riverside promenades, and clear blue skies. Rishikesh has some of the most off-beat experiences to offer like, white water rafting, cliff jumping, kayaking, body surfing, and many more!

Mussoorie

This is where the great writer Ruskin Bond used to live, and we could not better describe this place than him. Mussoorie is the most popular tourist destination in Uttarakhand among all kinds of travelers. Situated at a height of 1900 meters above sea level, Mussoorie was developed as a holiday paradise in the hills by the officers of the ruling East India Company. You can find more than 15 tourist attractions in Mussoorie at your disposal when you are planning to spend a few days in nature.

Kedarnath

Kedarnath is an ancient pilgrimage site popularly known to be built by the Pandava brothers from Mahabharata, and hence, it is a major pilgrimage for Hindu devotees. Kedarnath offers a magnificent view of the Himalayan ranges and is a beautiful place in Uttarakhand that you shouldn’t miss. The temperature here ranges from -2 degrees to 28 degrees, and the best time to visit Kedarnath is either May to June or September to October.

Almora

Located in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, Almora is home to unbeatable natural beauty. If you are looking to escape the scorching heat of the city, then Almora is a perfect place for you to seek refuge. Known for its religiously significant temples like Nanda Devi temple, Patal Devi temple, and Maa Dunagiri temple, Almora is one of the most important hill stations to visit in Uttarakhand for Hindu devotees. The lush greenery and divine beauty of this place will never seize a chance to amaze you.
